EXERCISES DURING PERIODS

Stretching

Gentle stretching helps relieve tension and improves flexibility. Focus on areas like your lower back and abdomen.

Yoga

Yoga poses, especially those emphasizing relaxation and gentle movements, can ease menstrual discomfort and stress.

Walking

Brisk walking boosts circulation, reduces bloating, and provides a low-impact cardiovascular exercise.

Pelvic Floor Exercises

Strengthening your pelvic floor muscles can help alleviate cramps and improve overall pelvic health.

Deep Breathing

Practicing deep breathing exercises helps relax your body and mind, reducing stress and promoting a sense of calmness.

Pilates

Pilates exercises focus on core strength and flexibility, providing relief from menstrual discomfort.

Cycling

Cycling is an excellent cardiovascular exercise that can improve blood flow and reduce menstrual cramps.

Strength Training

Incorporate light strength training to enhance muscle tone and reduce overall discomfort during periods.

DIET PLAN

Healthy Breakfast

Eat a balanced breakfast with whole grains, fruits, and protein to kickstart your day. For example, oatmeal with berries and a boiled egg.

Nutrient-Rich Snacks

Include snacks like nuts, yogurt, or fruits for sustained energy throughout the day. Almonds and Greek yogurt with honey are good options.

Lean Proteins

Incorporate lean proteins such as chicken, fish, tofu, or legumes for muscle support. Grilled chicken or a chickpea salad are healthy choices.

Hydration

Stay well-hydrated by drinking plenty of water, herbal teas, and infused water to reduce bloating. Aim for at least 8 glasses of water per day.

Leafy Greens

Include leafy greens like spinach and kale for iron and vitamins essential during menstruation. Try a spinach salad with mixed vegetables.

Whole Grains

Choose whole grains such as brown rice, quinoa, and oats for sustained energy and fiber. Quinoa bowl with vegetables is a nutritious option.

Fruits

Consume a variety of fruits for vitamins and natural sweetness. Apples, bananas, and berries make excellent choices for a snack or dessert.

Dairy or Dairy Alternatives

Include dairy or dairy alternatives for calcium. Greek yogurt, milk, or fortified plant-based milk are good sources.
